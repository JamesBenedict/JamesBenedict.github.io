James Benedict

tl;dr journalist who <3 computers will build you cool visuals

All the way back to my Microsoft Paint days I've been using computers to try to impress people. While I might long for those days of artistic liberty, now I'm producing more functional graphics for news organizations.

Currently I'm a graphics intern for the Wall Street Journal's Markets Desk. Before that I had a six month apprenticeship with the Reuters National Desk where we investigated the role Tasers played in more than a thousand deaths. In past career lives I wanted to be a chef, photographer or architect.

I like taking things apart and then seeing how I can put them back differently (I feel bad for some of my sister's barbies). Check out some of the more recent results below or on my github. Shoot me an email or tweet if you like anything you see.

About

We should probably get this out of the way: If you call me with an offer, I might respond by mimicking an airhorn. If I'm talking about something I love, you might need to ask me to slow down to catch every word. I'm an excitable person who isn't afraid to express my passions.

I'm attracted to technology because I love the rush of discovery and education. It is just as exhilarating to see how my code compiles as it was to boot the first computer I built. I promise if you let me into your system I won't break anything, but expect plenty of questions about how it works.

While technology fulfills my experimental interests, my passion is to bring more accountability into our society by creating clear journalism. Graphics can make the abstract tangible and a clear layout makes the complex approachable.

I'm a curious developer who enjoys experimentation almost as much as I love telling stories. My work as a designer, programmer and reporter allows me to empathize with the point of view of everyone on the team without losing sight of the reader. My specialties of user experience and interactive design let me make sure the reader never has to fight the story's medium to make sense of it.
