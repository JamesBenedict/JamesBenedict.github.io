tl;dr journalist who <3 computers will build you cool visuals

All the way back to my microsoft paint days I've been using computers to try to impress people. While I might long for those days of artistic liberty, now I'm producing more functional graphics for news organizations.

I'm working at Reuters on an investigative project I'd love to discuss after it publishes July 31. I graduated Indiana University with a Bachelors of Arts in Journalism and a Certificate (28 credits) in Informatics. In past career lives I have wanted to be a architect, chef or photographer.

I like taking things apart and then seeing how I can put them back differently (I feel bad for some of my sister's barbies). Check out some of the more recent results below or on my github. Shoot me an email or tweet if you like anything you see.

About

We should probably get this out of the way, if you call me with an offer I might respond by mimicking an airhorn. During the interview if you get me talking about something I like you might need me to slow down to understand every word. I'm an excitable person who is having a great time learning how to make better graphics.

I've never been good at staying satisfied with my current understanding of technology. I must have given myself half a dozen heart attacks learning how to jailbreak my phone or messing with my computer's BIOS, but I love the rush of discovery. I promise if you let me into your system I won't break anything but I might annoy you with how many questions I ask.

While soon I might be done with my schooling, I am far from being done with my education. I hope wherever I end up pushes me so I can keep learning better analysis and visualization methods. I hope to create as clean and intuitive of a design as I can.
